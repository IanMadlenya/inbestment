About

Overview

After the 2008 financial crisis, many Millennials are now more conservative with their financial planning. This usually results in poor long-term choices, such as keeping all assets in cash. Millennials have the most time to take advantage of market growth and rebound from market crashes, so we should be taking advantage of our longer time horizon.

Creating and maintaining a budget and getting out of debt should always be the top priority. However, the next step is to set longer-term financial goals, and there arenâ€™t enough easy-to-understand resources to help young people do this. For example, if you have saved up $10,000, where should you put it? Checkings, savings, company 401k, IRA, invest it?

The goal of Inbestment is to help individuals easily come up with a financial plan and determine the amount and type of accounts they should be funding.

Financial Assumptions

User has no debt. If user has debt, getting rid of debt should be prioritized first before using Inbestment. User must have at least $1 in assets to use Inbestment.

User has an income. Monthly expenses are being calculated as a percentage of income. If user has no income, please determine your annual expense and divide by 0.74 to use for the annual income.

Monthly expenses are being calculated as:

Monthly Income x
( 0.28 Housing Expense
+ 0.36 Misc/Non-housing Expense
+ 0.10 Cushion )

Checking account should be funded with at least one month's worth of expenses.
Savings account should be funded with at least three month's worth of expenses.

The 2014 and 2015 contribution limit for an IRA is $5,500. Inbestment assumes this base case for all users. However, there are many other options the user might want to consider. If the user is 50 or over, the contribution limit is $6,500. Millennials will want to consider the Roth IRA, which is contributed to with after-tax assets, but no tax liability on withdrawals. Be sure to check the income limit for qualification.
